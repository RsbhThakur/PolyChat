Explain the impact of quantum computing on encryption

Answer generated by GPT-5 Nano
Quantum computing represents a paradigm shift in computational power that threatens the security foundations of the modern internet. Its impact on encryption is primarily categorized into two areas: Symmetric Encryption and Asymmetric (Public-Key) Encryption.

1. Threat to Public-Key Cryptography
Most of the internet's security protocols (like HTTPS, SSL/TLS) rely on public-key algorithms such as RSA and Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC). These systems are based on mathematical problems (integer factorization and discrete logarithms) that are computationally infeasible for classical computers to solve.

Shor's Algorithm: A quantum algorithm capable of solving these specific mathematical problems exponentially faster than classical algorithms.
Consequence: A sufficiently powerful quantum computer could break current RSA-2048 encryption in hours or days, rendering digital signatures and secure communications obsolete.

2. Impact on Symmetric Encryption
Symmetric encryption (like AES-256) is generally more resilient to quantum attacks but not entirely immune.
Grover's Algorithm: Provides a quadratic speedup for searching unsorted databases. It effectively halves the bit-strength of symmetric keys.
Mitigation: Moving from AES-128 to AES-256 is largely considered sufficient to resist quantum attacks using Grover's algorithm.

The Solution: Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)
In response, NIST and other organizations are standardizing new cryptographic algorithms designed to be resistant to quantum computer attacks. This transition is known as "crypto-agility."
